Charles Darwin

12 February 1809 – 19 April 1882


"A man who dares to waste one hour of time has not discovered the value of life."


Here's a time line of Charles Darwins life:


  • 1809 - Born in Shrewsbury, England
  • 1818 - In June goes to Samuel Butler’s school at Shrewsbury as a boarder (stayed 7 years). Butler was the grandfather of Samuel Butler (1835-1902) the science writer and critic of Darwinism
  • 1826 - Takes a walking tour in North Wales. In the winter meets Dr Robert Grant (1793-1874), naturalist and Lamarckian, and examines marine animals. 10 November elected to Plinian Society.
  • 1829 - Travels to Wales to collect insects with entomologist Frederick William Hope. Spends Michaelmas term in Cambridge
  • 1831 - Meets Captain Robert FitzRoy (1805-65) and makes preparations for the voyage. Begins Beagle diary
  • 1835 - Beagle departs Lima on 7 September for the Galapagos Archipelago. Darwin spends 16 September to 20 October exploring the archipelago, 15-26 November in Tahiti, 21-30 November in New Zealand
  • 1836 - Beagle drops anchor at Falmouth, England, and on 4 October Darwin returns home to Shrewsbury. Begins to publish scientific papers
  • 1839 - Marries Emma Wedgwood on 29 January. Publishes Journal of Researches, later known as Voyage of the Beagle. Elected a Fellow of the Royal Society
  • 1858 - In June receives a letter from Alfred Russel Wallace who is collecting specimens in Indonesia. Wallace encloses an essay on species and varieties that mirrors Darwin’s own theory of natural selection
  • 1859 - On the Origin of Species by means of Natural Selection, or the Preservation of Favoured Races in the Struggle for Life is published in London on 24 November
  • 1864 - In November awarded the Copley medal of the Royal Society of London, its highest scientific honour
  • 1871 - Publishes "The Descent of Man, and Selection in relation to Sex"
  • 1882 - Dies 19 April, aged seventy-three

"It is not the strongest of the species that survives, nor the most intelligent that survives. It is the one that is most adaptable to change."

- Charles Darwin
